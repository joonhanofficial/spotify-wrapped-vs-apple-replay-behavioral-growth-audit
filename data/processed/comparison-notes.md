# Comparison Notes

## Purpose

This file converts raw source material into structured comparison notes. It is meant to help separate direct observation from interpretation before anything is turned into a stronger finding.

## Comparison Frame

### Area 1: Visible public circulation
- Spotify Wrapped: Appeared to generate strong recurring public visibility and broad social sharing during the year-end period.
- Apple Replay: Appeared noticeably less visible in public circulation despite offering a similar year-end recap format.
- Initial comparison note: The visible distribution gap appeared strong enough to support further comparison rather than being treated as a minor difference.
- Supporting evidence: Source 3, Third Actual Evidence Item, and Fourth Actual Evidence Item

### Area 2: Path from experience to sharing
- Spotify Wrapped: Appeared to move users from recap experience to public sharing with less visible friction and a more direct path to broadcast.
- Apple Replay: Appeared to introduce more distance between the recap experience and the act of public sharing.
- Initial comparison note: The likely difference was not just the existence of a recap feature, but how efficiently each experience converted private engagement into public posting.
- Supporting evidence: Source 2 and Second Actual Evidence Item

### Area 3: Share readiness of final output
- Spotify Wrapped: Appeared to present users with a more broadcast-ready output that felt naturally suited for social posting.
- Apple Replay: Appeared less immediately share-ready, which may have reduced how easily users turned the recap into public-facing content.
- Initial comparison note: The final output itself likely mattered. A recap experience may generate interest, but stronger share readiness can make that interest easier to convert into visible circulation.
- Supporting evidence: Source 2 and Second Actual Evidence Item
