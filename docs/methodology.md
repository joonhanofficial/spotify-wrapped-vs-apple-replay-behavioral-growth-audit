# Methodology

## Approach

This case was approached as a comparative behavioral growth audit rather than a platform-level performance analysis. Because internal product, engagement, and acquisition data were not available, the analysis relied on public signals, product-flow observation, and distribution proxies to examine why Spotify Wrapped appeared to generate stronger visible sharing than Apple Replay.

The purpose was not to prove exact platform-level causality, but to build a structured explanation for the visible distribution gap between two similar year-end recap experiences.

## Comparison Focus

The comparison centered on three main layers:

1. visible distribution outcome  
2. likely friction points within each user journey  
3. broader business implications of how product design may influence sharing efficiency

This helped keep the case focused on behavioral mechanics rather than turning it into a broad brand comparison.

## Evidence Types Used

The analysis used three main evidence types:

### 1. Public distribution signals

These were used to assess whether one product appeared to generate stronger visible circulation than the other.

Examples include:
- visible social posting volume
- recurring public attention
- search interest patterns
- platform-level public conversation or sharing presence

### 2. Product-flow observation

This was used to compare how easily each product moved a user from private consumption to public sharing.

The review focused on:
- access path
- number of user steps
- clarity of the share moment
- broadcast readiness of the final output
- how naturally the experience invited social posting

### 3. Distribution proxies

Because direct internal sharing and acquisition data were unavailable, proxy signals were used to support directional interpretation.

These proxies were not treated as exact performance measures. Instead, they were used to assess whether one experience appeared more structurally capable of converting user interest into public distribution.

## Analytical Logic

The analysis followed this logic:

1. Identify the visible distribution gap between Spotify Wrapped and Apple Replay.  
2. Confirm that the gap appeared meaningful enough to investigate.  
3. Compare the likely user journey and sharing flow of each product.  
4. Identify where friction may interrupt conversion from experience to broadcast.  
5. Interpret how lower or higher sharing friction may affect amplification.  
6. Connect that amplification difference to broader business implications such as reach efficiency and growth leverage.

## Scope Boundaries

This project did not attempt to quantify:
- exact internal share rates
- exact user conversion rates
- exact acquisition impact
- exact financial return generated by either product

Instead, the project focused on whether the structure of each experience could plausibly help explain the visible difference in public spread.

## Why This Method Was Chosen

A traditional performance analysis was not possible without internal platform data. A behavioral audit was a more suitable approach because the case began with a visible real-world pattern: one recap experience spread heavily in public, while the other appeared far more muted.

Given that starting point, the most useful analytical route was to examine whether differences in friction, user flow, and share readiness could help explain that outcome.

## Interpretation Standard

Throughout this case, findings were treated with three levels of confidence:

### Direct observation
What could be clearly seen from public product behavior, sharing presence, or visible user journey structure.

### Supported inference
What could be reasonably concluded by combining public evidence with product-flow comparison.

### Cautious implication
What the observed patterns may suggest for distribution efficiency or business impact, without claiming exact measurable outcomes.

## Method Limitation

This methodology is useful for structured comparison, but it does not provide experimental proof. It is strongest as a comparative behavioral explanation, not as a definitive causal measurement model.
