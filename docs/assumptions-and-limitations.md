# Assumptions and Limitations

## Why This Section Matters

This case study is strongest as a structured behavioral explanation of a visible distribution gap. It is not a controlled experiment, and it does not rely on internal platform data. Because of that, the credibility of the case depends partly on being clear about what was assumed, what was directly observed, and what remains uncertain.

## Core Assumptions

### 1. Visible public sharing reflects a meaningful distribution difference

A central assumption in this case is that the difference in visible public circulation between Spotify Wrapped and Apple Replay was meaningful enough to justify analysis.

### 2. Product-flow design influences share conversion

This case assumes that the structure of a user journey can affect whether private engagement turns into public posting. In other words, the path between experience and broadcast is treated as behaviorally important, not just cosmetically different.

### 3. Public signals can support directional interpretation

Because internal metrics were unavailable, the analysis assumes that public signals such as visible sharing behavior, recurring attention, and search presence can still support a meaningful directional reading, even if they cannot provide exact performance measures.

## What Was Not Assumed

This project did not assume:
- that Spotify Wrapped's stronger visibility was caused by product-flow design alone
- that public sharing perfectly represents total user engagement
- that Apple Replay had low user value simply because it appeared less visible
- that visible distribution automatically translates into exact financial return

## Main Limitations

### 1. No internal platform data

The biggest limitation is the absence of internal data such as:
- share rates
- conversion rates
- user retention
- acquisition lift
- downstream revenue impact

Without this data, the case cannot quantify exact performance differences.

### 2. Public signals are imperfect proxies

Public visibility, search interest, and observable sharing activity are useful, but incomplete. They indicate pattern and direction more than full scale. They are best treated as supporting evidence, not exact measurement.

### 3. Causality cannot be isolated

Even if product friction appears to matter, it is unlikely to be the only factor. Other possible influences include:
- brand affinity
- existing cultural momentum
- user demographics
- established sharing habits
- broader platform positioning

This means the case cannot claim that flow design alone caused the full distribution gap.

### 4. Commercial implications are directional, not exact

The business meaning of this case is strongest at the level of strategic implication. It supports the idea that better share conversion may improve reach efficiency and organic momentum. It does not prove exact acquisition value or financial return.

## Confidence Boundaries

The strongest parts of this case are:
- identifying the visible distribution gap
- comparing likely friction points in each user journey
- explaining why share readiness may matter for amplification

The weaker parts of this case are:
- any exact performance interpretation
- any exact causal attribution
- any exact financial implication

## Final Limitation Statement

This project should be read as a structured comparative audit, not as definitive proof of internal platform performance. Its value lies in disciplined interpretation rather than precise measurement.
